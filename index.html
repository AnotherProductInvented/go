Objective

The goal of the game is to control more territory on the board than your opponent by surrounding empty intersections with your stones.

Setup

  • Board: A 19×19 grid.
  • Players: Two players – Black (plays first) and White.
  • Stones: Players take turns placing stones on the intersections (not inside squares).

Game Rules

  • Players take turns placing one stone per turn on an empty intersection.
  • Once placed, stones do not move.
  • A group of connected stones (horizontally or vertically) must have at least one empty adjacent intersection ("liberty").
  • If a group loses all liberties (is completely surrounded by the opponent’s stones), it is captured and removed from the board.
  • Ko Rule: You cannot make a move that recreates the previous board state. If you try, a fancy message will appear.
  • Suicide Rule: You cannot place a stone if that move would leave your stone (or group) with no liberties, unless it captures opponent stones. A message will alert you if this happens.
  • Passing Turns: A player may pass if no beneficial move is available. Two consecutive passes end the game.

Scoring & Winning

  • Territory Points: Each empty intersection completely enclosed by your stones counts as a point.
  • Captured Stones: Each captured enemy stone counts as one point.
  • Komi (White’s Bonus): White receives an extra 7 points to offset Black's first-move advantage.

The player with the highest total (territory + captured stones + komi) wins.
